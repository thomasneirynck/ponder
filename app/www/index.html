How does it work?

Ponder uses a neural networked called a "self-organizing map" to layout the data in two dimensions. Items that are similar are mapped close to each other, while items that are dissimilar are positioned further away.

Similarity is determined by calculating the distance between two rows of data. Both fields with ordinal data, as well as categorical data, are taken into account.
