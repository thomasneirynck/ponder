Iris Flowers

The famous iris dataset: 150 samples, divided equally into three classes. Four features where measured of each sample; the length and width of the petals, and the length and width of the samples.

The three types of flowers each occupy a region on the map. Their position is based on their shared characteristics. The four measurements are sufficient to place flowers of the same type together on the map, without taking into account the type of flower.

This dataset contains only two obvious clusters that can clearly be distinguished on the map, even without any extra labeling.

Source: Wikipedia

Titanic Passengers

A list of passengers of the Titantic, organized by various attributes: their gender, their age, whether they traveled in first, second and 3rd, class, where they embarked, and whether they survived the voyage.

You can use the map to gain valuable insight in which type of passengers survived, and who was not as fortunate. Particularly, woman had a much higher chance of survival then men. Can you find other relationships?

Source: Kaggle

2015 NBA Playoffs

Players and their statitics for the 2015 playoffs. This includes their position, age, minutes player per game, two and tree point averages and more.

Players with similar statistics are positioned together on the map. Note for example how star players Stephen Curry, Lebron James, and James Harden are all positioned next to each other, even though they play different positions.

Source: NBA Stuffer

Frequently Asked Questions

How are the maps created?

Ponder uses an unsupervised machine learning technique called "self-organizing maps" that can be used to generate two-dimensional layouts of multidimensional data. It is unsupervised since the technique attempts to detect clusters in the data without any human intervention. All that is required from the user is to indicate what variables (ie. "columns") should be taken into account.

What amount of data can ponder handle?

The amount of data that can be mapped depends on the number of rows and columns in the spreadsheet, the number of unique values in the columns, and on how powerful your computer is. Broadly speaking, Ponder can handle small to moderately sized files.
